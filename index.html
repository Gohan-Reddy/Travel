Tourist Places

Chilika Lake is the largest brackish water lagoon in Asia and second largest coastal lagoon in the world, spread over the Puri, Khordha and Ganjam districts of Odisha state on the east coast of India, at the mouth of the Daya River, flowing into the Bay of Bengal, covering an area of over 1,100 square kilometres.

9 mins

Cuttack is a city in the eastern Indian state of Odisha. It's on a peninsula flanked by the Mahanadi River to the north and the Kathajodi River to the south. The ruins of moated Barabati Fort, once home to the Eastern Ganga dynasty, show the city’s medieval significance. Within the fort's walls lies the Shahi Mosque, with its 3 white domes.

9 mins

Satkosia is a wildlife sanctuary and tiger reserve in the state of Odisha, India. The sanctuary was established in 1976 and is named after the narrow stretch of the Mahanadi River, which is seven miles long. The sanctuary is located at the meeting point of the Deccan Peninsula and the Eastern Ghats, and is home to a variety of plants and animals.

9 mins

Bhitarkanika Mangroves is a mangrove wetland in Odisha, India, covering an area of 650 km in the Brahmani River and Baitarani River deltas.

9 mins

Bhubaneswar is an ancient city in India’s eastern state of Odisha, formerly Orissa. Many temples built from sandstone are dotted around Bindu Sagar Lake in the old city, including the 11th-century Hindu Lingaraja Temple. Outside Rajarani Temple are sculpted figures of the guardians of the 8 cardinal and ordinal directions.

9 mins

Berhampur, also known as Brahmapur, is a city on the eastern coast of Odisha, India. It is the oldest city in the Ganjam District and is a trading center for southern Odisha. The city is nicknamed "The Silk City".

9 mins

Puri is a coastal city and a municipality in the state of Odisha in eastern India. It is the district headquarters of Puri district and is situated on the Bay of Bengal, 60 kilometres south of the state capital of Bhubaneswar.
